SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction)
SMILE LASIK (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) is an advanced, minimally invasive
vision correction surgery that aims to treat refractive errors such as myopia (nearsightedness)
and astigmatism. Unlike traditional LASIK surgery, which involves creating a flap in the cornea
to reshape it, SMILE LASIK uses a small incision (usually around 2-4 millimeters) to extract a
lenticule (a small, disc-shaped piece of tissue) from the cornea. This process reshapes the cornea
and improves its focusing ability. SMILE LASIK has gained popularity because of its precision,
faster recovery time, and lower risk of complications compared to traditional LASIK.
Symptoms and Causes
Symptoms that indicate a need for refractive surgery, such as SMILE LASIK, include:
Blurry vision: Difficulty seeing clearly at distances, which is commonly seen in myopia
(nearsightedness).
Frequent changes in glasses or contact lens prescription: If the prescription continues
to change rapidly, it may indicate a need for corrective surgery.
Eye strain or headaches: Prolonged eye strain due to refractive errors can lead to
headaches or discomfort, particularly when reading or using digital devices.
Difficulty seeing at night: People with refractive errors may experience challenges with
night vision, especially when driving in low-light conditions.
Common causes of refractive errors that SMILE LASIK treats include:

<!-- Page 567 -->
Myopia (Nearsightedness): The most common cause of refractive errors, where distant
objects appear blurry while close-up objects are clear. It occurs when the cornea is too
curved or the eye is too long.
Astigmatism: A condition where the cornea has an irregular shape, causing blurry or
distorted vision at all distances.
Hyperopia (Farsightedness): Less common than myopia, where close-up objects are
blurry, often caused by a cornea that is too flat or the eye being too short.
Diagnosis and Tests
Before undergoing SMILE LASIK surgery, a comprehensive eye exam is conducted to
determine the patient’s eligibility for the procedure:
Eye refraction test: Determines the exact prescription for glasses or contact lenses to
assess the degree of myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism.
Corneal topography: Maps the surface of the cornea to check for any irregularities or
abnormalities. This helps the surgeon understand the cornea’s shape and thickness, which
is crucial for planning the surgery.
Pachymetry: Measures the thickness of the cornea to ensure that it is thick enough to
undergo the procedure safely.
Slit-lamp examination: A detailed eye examination that checks for other issues, such as
dry eyes or signs of cataracts, which may affect surgery eligibility.
Wavefront analysis: This test creates a detailed map of how light enters your eyes,
helping to detect imperfections in the cornea that may contribute to vision problems.
Management and Treatment
In many cases, refractive errors are initially treated with glasses or contact lenses to correct
vision. However, when these methods become inconvenient or ineffective, SMILE LASIK
offers a permanent solution for correcting the refractive errors.
Treatment for candidates who are eligible for SMILE LASIK involves:
Pre-surgery consultation: Detailed assessment to ensure that the patient’s eyes are
healthy and the cornea is suitable for the surgery.
Refractive error correction: SMILE LASIK uses precise laser technology to create a
lenticule within the cornea. The lenticule is then extracted through a small incision,
which reshapes the cornea and improves its focusing ability.
Post-surgery care: Eye drops, typically anti-inflammatory or antibiotic eye drops, are
prescribed to help with healing. Patients are also advised to avoid rubbing their eyes and
to rest them for several days after surgery.
SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) Types & Surgery
There is typically only one type of SMILE LASIK, but variations may occur based on the
specific needs of the patient:

<!-- Page 568 -->
1. Standard SMILE LASIK: The most common procedure, where a small incision is
made, and the lenticule is extracted from the cornea to treat myopia and astigmatism.
2. Customized SMILE LASIK: This variation allows for a more personalized treatment
based on the individual’s unique eye structure. Wavefront technology is used to create a
highly customized treatment plan, providing a higher degree of precision for patients with
complex refractive errors.
The SMILE LASIK procedure steps include:
1. Preparation: The patient is given a mild sedative and numbing eye drops to ensure
comfort. The surgeon uses a femtosecond laser to create a small lenticule inside the
cornea.
2. Incision creation: A small, precise incision is made in the cornea, and the lenticule is
extracted. This reshapes the cornea and improves its focusing ability.
3. Post-surgery care: The patient is monitored for a brief period before being discharged,
with clear instructions for recovery.
The procedure usually takes about 20-30 minutes per eye, and most patients experience only
minimal discomfort during the process.
Complicated SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction)
Though SMILE LASIK is generally safe, complications can arise:
Dry eyes: A temporary increase in dry eye symptoms is common after SMILE LASIK
surgery, but it typically resolves over time.
Infection: As with any surgical procedure, there is a risk of infection, though it is rare.
Proper aftercare and hygiene can minimize this risk.
Undercorrection or overcorrection: In some cases, the refractive error may not be fully
corrected, requiring additional treatments or enhancements.
Visual disturbances: Some patients may experience glare, halos, or double vision,
particularly at night. These symptoms are usually temporary but may persist in a small
number of patients.
Flap complications (rare in SMILE LASIK): Traditional LASIK surgery involves
creating a flap in the cornea, and although SMILE LASIK avoids this, any incisions in
the cornea can still cause complications.
SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) in Adults
SMILE LASIK is typically performed on adults aged 18 and older, who have stable refractive
errors. It is ideal for those who are tired of the inconvenience of glasses or contact lenses and
want a more permanent solution. The surgery is safe and effective for correcting myopia,
astigmatism, and mild hyperopia.
Adult candidates should have:

<!-- Page 569 -->
Stable vision: The refractive error should not be changing significantly. The eyes should
be stable for at least one year before surgery.
Healthy eyes: Patients should have no eye diseases such as cataracts, glaucoma, or
severe dry eye disease.
Good overall health: Certain medical conditions like autoimmune diseases can affect
healing and may require special consideration.
SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction) in Children
SMILE LASIK is not commonly recommended for children since their eyes are still
developing. Vision prescription may change frequently in young individuals, and their corneas
may not yet be stable enough for permanent surgery. The American Academy of Ophthalmology
generally suggests that refractive surgery like SMILE LASIK should be postponed until the
child’s eye development has stabilized, typically around the age of 18.
Prevention
Although SMILE LASIK surgery can correct refractive errors, preventing the need for surgery
involves:
Regular eye exams: Early detection of refractive errors can help manage vision problems
before they become significant.
Proper eye care: Protecting your eyes from trauma or injury (such as wearing safety
goggles during sports) and avoiding harmful UV light can preserve eye health.
Healthy lifestyle: A balanced diet and maintaining overall health can contribute to better
vision over time.
Outlook / Prognosis
The prognosis for patients undergoing SMILE LASIK is excellent. Most people experience a
significant improvement in vision, with the ability to perform daily activities without the need
for glasses or contact lenses. Recovery time is typically fast, with many patients noticing
improved vision within a few days, though full stabilization may take a few weeks. SMILE
LASIK is a safe and effective option for vision correction, with a high success rate and a low
complication rate.
Living With SMILE LASIK Eye Surgery
After SMILE LASIK, patients should follow the aftercare instructions to ensure proper healing.
This includes:
Using prescribed eye drops: Antibiotic and anti-inflammatory drops are essential to
prevent infection and manage inflammation.
Avoiding eye strain: It is important to rest the eyes, especially during the initial recovery
period.

Protecting the eyes: Wearing sunglasses and avoiding direct sunlight exposure can help
protect the healing eyes.
Routine follow-ups: Regular check-ups with the eye surgeon are essential to monitor
healing and address any concerns.
Most patients can return to their normal activities within a few days, but strenuous activities like
swimming or heavy exercise should be avoided for a few weeks.
Additional Common Questions (FAQs)
Q1: How long does SMILE LASIK last?
SMILE LASIK results are permanent, but refractive errors may change over time due to natural
aging processes, such as presbyopia (difficulty focusing on near objects).
Q2: Does SMILE LASIK hurt?
The surgery itself is painless, thanks to numbing eye drops. Post-surgery discomfort is typically
minimal, with some patients experiencing mild dryness or irritation.
Q3: How soon can I return to work after SMILE LASIK?
Most patients can return to work within 1-2 days after surgery, though those with strenuous jobs
may need a little more time to rest.
Q4: Can I have SMILE LASIK if I have astigmatism?
Yes, SMILE LASIK is effective for treating both myopia and astigmatism. It reshapes the cornea
to improve focus and vision clarity.
Q5: Is SMILE LASIK safer than traditional LASIK?
Both procedures are considered safe, but SMILE LASIK has a lower risk of complications such
as flap-related issues. It also typically offers faster recovery and less post-surgery dryness.
Q6: Am I a good candidate for SMILE LASIK?
Candidates should be over 18, have stable refractive errors, and have healthy eyes. A detailed
consultation with an eye surgeon will help determine your eligibility.
