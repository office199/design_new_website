Orthoptek
Orthoptek is an advanced medical technology device designed to treat certain types of glaucoma,
primarily primary open-angle glaucoma. It utilizes precise laser treatment to improve the eye’s
natural fluid drainage, thereby lowering intraocular pressure (IOP) without surgery or
medications. Orthoptek offers a minimally invasive approach aimed at preserving vision and
preventing glaucoma progression.
Symptoms and Causes
Symptoms Addressed by Orthoptek
Gradual loss of peripheral vision (tunnel vision)
Eye discomfort or mild pain
Headaches
Blurred or hazy vision in advanced stages
Causes of Glaucoma Treated with Orthoptek
Blockage or reduced function of the trabecular meshwork, the eye’s drainage system
Increased intraocular pressure damaging the optic nerve
Primary open-angle glaucoma, where the drainage angle remains open but fluid outflow
is impaired
Orthoptek targets the underlying drainage issue to control pressure buildup.
Diagnosis and Tests
Diagnosis of glaucoma requiring Orthoptek treatment involves comprehensive eye evaluation,
including:
Tonometry: Measures intraocular pressure.
Gonioscopy: Examines the drainage angle of the eye.
Visual field testing: Assesses peripheral vision loss.

Optic nerve imaging: Uses OCT or photography to detect nerve damage.
Pachymetry: Measures corneal thickness, influencing IOP readings.
These tests help determine if Orthoptek is appropriate.
Management and Treatment
Orthoptek treatment uses a laser to precisely target and stimulate the trabecular meshwork,
enhancing fluid drainage and lowering IOP.
It is an outpatient procedure, usually lasting 15-20 minutes.
Performed under topical anesthesia, it is painless and quick.
Often used as a primary treatment or adjunct to medication.
Minimal side effects, with rare risks including transient inflammation or IOP spikes.
Post-procedure, patients are monitored regularly to track IOP and optic nerve health.
Orthoptek Types & Surgery
Orthoptek represents a specific category of selective laser trabeculoplasty (SLT) devices
designed for precision and efficiency.
It is non-surgical but offers similar IOP-lowering benefits to trabeculectomy surgery
with fewer risks.
Can be repeated if needed.
Often preferred over invasive surgery as a first-line or adjunct treatment.
There are no distinct "types" of Orthoptek, but it fits within broader glaucoma laser therapy
options.
Prevention
While glaucoma cannot always be prevented, timely diagnosis and treatment like Orthoptek help
slow progression and preserve vision.
Preventive Strategies:
Regular comprehensive eye exams, especially after age 40 or with family history
Managing systemic health issues like hypertension and diabetes
Avoiding eye trauma and protecting eyes during activities
Adherence to prescribed glaucoma treatments and monitoring

Early intervention is key to preventing irreversible vision loss.
Outlook / Prognosis
The prognosis following Orthoptek treatment is generally favorable:
Many patients achieve significant and sustained IOP reduction.
Risk of progression slows, preserving remaining vision.
Some may reduce or stop glaucoma medications.
Periodic follow-ups are essential to monitor efficacy and need for retreatment.
Outcomes depend on glaucoma severity and adherence to follow-up care.
Living with Orthoptek Treatment
Post-treatment, normal activities can typically be resumed quickly.
Eye drops or anti-inflammatory medications may be prescribed temporarily.
Patients should attend regular eye check-ups to monitor IOP.
Awareness of symptoms like sudden vision changes or eye pain is important for prompt
response.
Orthoptek enables many patients to better manage glaucoma with minimal disruption to lifestyle.
Additional Common Questions
Is Orthoptek painful?
No, the procedure is painless due to topical anesthesia.
How soon does Orthoptek work?
IOP reduction is often noticeable within weeks.
Can Orthoptek replace glaucoma medications?
It can reduce or sometimes eliminate the need, but some patients may still require drops.
Are there any side effects?
Side effects are minimal but can include temporary eye redness or pressure spikes.
Is Orthoptek suitable for all glaucoma types?
Primarily for open-angle glaucoma; angle-closure glaucoma usually requires different
treatments.
FAQs

Q1. How long does Orthoptek treatment last?
A: The laser procedure takes about 15-20 minutes and is done in-office.
Q2. Can Orthoptek be repeated?
A: Yes, it can be repeated if intraocular pressure rises again.
Q3. Does insurance cover Orthoptek?
A: Many insurance plans cover laser glaucoma treatments; check with your provider.
Q4. What happens if Orthoptek fails?
A: Other treatments, including medications or surgery, may be considered.
Q5. How often should I get follow-ups after Orthoptek?
A: Typically, every 3 to 6 months or as recommended by your eye doctor.
Orthoptek offers a safe, effective, and minimally invasive option for managing glaucoma,
helping preserve vision and improve quality of life. If you have glaucoma or are at risk, consult
your eye care professional about whether Orthoptek is right for you.
